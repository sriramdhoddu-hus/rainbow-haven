Why This Happens

LGBTQIA+ youth experiencing housing instability often face rejection from family due to their sexual orientation or gender identity. Many are kicked out, or decide to run away from home after coming out, which is worsened by lack of affirming support systems. Discrimination in shelters and a lack of awareness on shelters aggravate the issue, leaving thousands without safe shelter each year.


Contributing factors include societal stigma, inadequate protection of queer individuals from the government, and limited access to affirming resources in many communities.

Why This Matters

Addressing housing instability for LGBTQIA+ youth is crucial for their safety, mental health, and future opportunities. Unstable housing leads to higher risks of trauma, substance use, and homelessness cycles that persist into adulthood.


Supportive shelters and communities aid in building resilience, foster community, and open up doors for housing and financial stability, benefiting society as a whole.
