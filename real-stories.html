Real Stories

Story #1 - Alan Turing

Alan Turing was treated unjustly due to his sexual orientation at a time when it was criminalised in the UK. In 1952, he was punished and forced to go through medical treatment that harmed his body. Despite his unfair treatment, his earlier work helped Britain win World War II. Today, he is recognised as an important scientist, and people now recognize how unjustly he was treated.

Story #2 - Conan Gray

Conan Gray grew up in Texas—a highly conservative state against homosexuality and transgenderism. He often felt out of place and was judged and teased by others for not fitting the traditional norms of masculinity. Despite this, Gray went onto become a well-known pop artist, with songs for people with similar experiences, such as 'Found Heaven'.

Story #3 - Elton John

Elton John experienced intense public and media homophobia during a time when being openly gay was widely stigmatized, forcing him to navigate his identity while being judged, while also dealing with personal struggles. Over time, he embraced his sexuality openly, overcame addiction, and built a stable and celebrated career, using his fame to advocate for LGBTQ+ rights and support those affected by HIV/AIDS.

Story #4 - SOPHIE

SOPHIE was a transgender woman who faced judgment and misunderstanding in both the music industry and society because of her gender identity, and experienced heavy transphobia from society. Despite this, she became a groundbreaking producer, praised for her innovative music and inspiring LGBTQIA+ artists around the world, showing how she turned challenges into influence and resilience.
